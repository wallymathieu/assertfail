We dont like to hear other people talk about their roleplaying characters. We do not like to hear about people telling us about their imaginary friends great adventure.

We live in a time of stories. Companies sell fables and are not shy about it.

> The troll of Eftlav helped with building the towers of stone and sand at the edge of the Årsta bay. He was paid in gold to raise the quay.

> Because of the office elfs, we no longer need anyone to manually take care of the paperwork. I no longer write things by hand like a cave man.

> Garm drank the river of Flint to cool his appetite for tales. What whispers told by fishes did his toungue lap up? 

What stories do we tell each other? Who gets to tell the stories? Who do we listen to?

The oracles from the west tell of many wondrous things. They tell of want and plenty, they tell of gold and of gravel.

> I reject your reality and substitute my own.

Was funny when told by Adam Savage. Now, it takes on darker tones. Understanding of reality bends and warps in the presence of money and power. Where is the counter culture?

I use the tools. I try to learn how best to apply them. 

Some people seem to start out with a reasonable take on how to use the tools, but then veer off by telling us that agentic loop engineering makes sense. Maybe it is part of the attention economy, where the need to be seen trumps saying useful things?

I do not like the soothsayers of AI. The stories told by them make me hope that they, like the ranks of Sam Bankman-Fried, Elizabeth Holmes et.c., be prosecuted for their grifts.
